Fat Woman Fitness

Where it Began

It all really began when I was a child. I spent most of my free-time on my bicycle. I spent hours riding through the countryside, tearing up miles of backroads, cutting through streams, valleys, and curves. On my bike, I was safe and all was right in the world. Although the tan I had from back then has faded, the strength and yearning for freedom never has. By the time I became a young adult, that once monumental joy for riding, went dorment. Slowly, I stopped moving, withdrew into depression and morphed into a morbidly obese adult.

Where I Went

In 2017 I went on a strict diet and lost 85 pounds. Although I seemed happy, I was incredibly weak and unhealthy. I wasn't eating enough of the right things and consequently I lost a tremendous amount of muscle. My doctor recommended I begin weight lifting. After a few months of training I began to experience the flame of athleticism. I felt powerful and capable. Eventually the strictness faded, giving way to old habits that allowed the weight to quickly return with a vegence. As so many women can attest to, the weight came back and then some. That's when I realized something had to change. Following strict diets and waiting for my perfect body was only ever going to lead to failure. I began to see my body in a whole new light. I was no longer willing to wait for fitness to arrive before enjoying movement. I needed to learn how to enjoy movement in the body I already had.

By May 2022, I was pushing 300 pounds and movement was painful. I decided enough was enough and I took my first walk in ages. I had found a gloriously secluded trail at a local park. Being .75 mi, no one took the trail. One morning, after a fresh rain, I went for my walk. Halfway through my trail I ran into a massive swarm of gnats. I couldn't breathe without inhaling them. In a fit of rage, I took off running. As long as I was running I wasn't being bothered so I tried to keep running. Shortly into my run I felt this surge, this tremendous high. It took me 34 years to realize I was a lucky few who actually experienced runners high. I was sold in that moment. Then the rain set it. One rainy day, I found myself running a loop through my house shouting "No excuses!" I ran 1 mile in my house that day. I knew I needed something new. On May 15, 2022, I bought my first treadmill. On May 16th, my doctor told me I had early onset arthritis in my knees and ankles and stressed that I shouldn't use a treadmill or even walk as a form of exercise. A few tears later, I realized this was not the end for me. I had found something I just couldn't live without.

Where I am

After my diagnosis, I dove full tilt into my fitness. I found a sport that had all the activities I loved. I found the sport of triathlon. A triathlon consists of three sports:

  • Swimming
  • Biking
  • Running
