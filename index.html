Thoughts on Progressive Enhancement

Progressive enhancement essentially means that webpages are displayed in layers. Content is first, then CSS, followed by JavaScript. Progressive enhancement is taking content and displaying it depending on the limitations the viewer may have. For some people, all they can handle is the content. Whether they are on an older computer or have settings that donâ€™t allow for a more immersive browsing experience, having the option to view the content alone allows them to engage despite their limitations. Sometimes a viewer may be able to handle some but not all display types. For example, some may not have the support needed to display JavaScript but could handle most CSS. Therefore, the separation is important as is the content first perspective. This perspective provides compatibility and stability for all users. Without it we are left to the limitations of Graceful Degradation, which refers to a break in functionality that brings the entire system down.
