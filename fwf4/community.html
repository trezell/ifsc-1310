About the Community

The triathlon community is full of people from all walks of life. There are all types of people at all types of fitness levels. Because the sport of triathlon is so trying and takes a great deal of preparation, there is so much mutual respect between triathletes.

I strongly encourage joining a local triathlon group. The benefits vary by group but often include savings at local sporting stores, special (sometimes free) training sessions, and practice course information. My local triathlon group gives out discount cards to their members, as well as free merchandise and entrance to a private lake for open water swimming practice. I've also received a free gait analysis and plan to take part in next season's stroke analysis. Additionally, members meet frequently to run, ride, and swim together. This really comes in handy when you struggle to get those last few miles!

Having a community is so very helpful. Training can get a little boring after a while and having people around you to make your training entertaining can make a huge difference. In fact, several triathlon veterans wonder if they could have made it as far as they have if they hadn't had the support they did. Community is so very important and I encourage every interested athlete to investigate to find their own community.

But what if you don't have a group near you? Can you still develop a community to help you achieve your goals? Absolutely! In fact, the vast majority of my support comes from the internet. There are numerous facebook groups and subreddit groups that are super helpful and inviting and unlocks the chances of finding other fat triathletes. Yes. We are out there! You are not the only one!!
